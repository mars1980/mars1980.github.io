Please Note: Course schedule subject to change.

Week 1:
  • Introductions
  • Class Overview
  • Class Exercise: Look, I made this for you...
  • Readings for next week: Alfred North Whitehead - selections from Process and Reality, Lucretius - selections from On the Nature of Things, Hegel, Descartes
Week 2:
  • Discussion: Conceptions of Space, Pre-Modernity; History of Cartography/Geography, Development of 2 point perspective, Euclidean Space, Materialsm, Idealsim, Dialectics, and Buddhism
  • Readings for next week: Henri Lefebvre, Guy Debord, Marx...
  • First Experiment: Pscyhogeographical Map
Week 3:
  • Discussion: Post War France - Reconfiguring and Questioning Space
  • Readings for next week: Trevor Paglan, Experimental Geography
Week 4:
  • Discussion: F#@K The Gallery: Critical Spatial Practice / Toward a New Public Art
  • Informal Critique: Psychogeographical Map
Week 5:
  • Discussion: The Personal is Political: Bodies and Power (queer space, black space, family, religion, intimacy)
  • Second Experiment: Sound Map of Intimate or 'Othered' Space
Week 6:
  • Discussion: Tactical + Locative Media, Guest Speaker: Josh Begley (artist)
  • Informal Critique: Sound Maps
Week 7:
  • Discussion: Virtual Space and Human Interaction, Guest Speaker: Stanford VR Lab
  • Third Assignment: 'Virtual' Occupation
Week 8:
  • Guest Lecture: Jacob Kang Brown (sociologist)
  • Informal Critique: Virtual Occupation
Week 9:
  • Discussion: Artists in the Urban Environment, Urban Exploration and Movement, Gentrification
  • Discussion: Final Project Proposals
Week 10:
  • Discussion: Land Art and 'The Environment'
  • Discussion: Final Project Status Updates
Week 11:
  • Final project in class workshop
Week 12:
  • Final Project Critiques - w/ Guests
